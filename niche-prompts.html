Pain Point Mining — Reddit & Forums
Симулира анализ на Reddit жалби, за да намери реални болезнени точки.
You are a market researcher specializing in finding SaaS opportunities from online complaints. Analyze common pain points that professionals in [industry: e.g. accounting, real estate, construction] frequently complain about on Reddit, LinkedIn, and industry forums. For each pain point: - Quote a realistic complaint (what they actually say) - Rate urgency (1-10) - Rate willingness to pay (1-10) - Existing solutions and their failures - SaaS opportunity that could solve it - Estimated monthly budget this persona has for software Focus on problems where existing software is "too expensive", "too complex", or "doesn't exist in our language/market".

5-минутен SaaS Validator
Дай идея → получи честна оценка дали си струва да строиш.
You are a brutally honest SaaS advisor who has seen 1000+ startup ideas fail and succeed. Evaluate this SaaS idea: [describe your idea in 2-3 sentences] Score it on: 1. MARKET SIZE: TAM estimate with reasoning 2. PAIN SEVERITY: Is this a painkiller or vitamin? (1-10) 3. COMPETITION: Who exists, what's the gap? 4. TIMING: Why now? Why not 3 years ago? 5. SOLO FOUNDER REALITY: Can 1 dev build MVP in 6 weeks? 6. PATH TO $5K MRR: How many customers, what sales channel? End with: VERDICT (Build it / Test it first / Avoid) and the single biggest reason for your verdict. Be ruthless. Don't sugarcoat.
Claude / ChatGPT

Take the broad niche: [e.g. "project management software"] Break it down into 15 specific micro-niches that are: - Underserved (existing tools don't focus on them) - Have a clear, specific user persona - Large enough to support a $10K MRR SaaS For each micro-niche provide: - Niche name (specific) - Persona: "A [job title] at a [company type] who needs to [specific workflow]" - Why generic tools fail them - 1 feature that would make them switch immediately - Price they'd pay per month - How to find the first 10 customers Sort by easiest to hardest to reach customers.

EMOTIONAL JOB: How do they want to feel while doing it? SOCIAL JOB: How do they want to be perceived? CURRENT WORKAROUND: How are they doing it now (be specific)? FAILURE MODES: What goes wrong with the current approach? SAAS OPPORTUNITY: 1-sentence product description BUYER vs USER: Who pays vs who uses it? BUDGET SIGNAL: What do they already spend money on that's adjacent?

I'm researching the competitive landscape for [niche: e.g. "invoice automation for small businesses"]. Analyze the top 5-8 existing solutions in this space. For each: - Name, pricing, target customer - Their #1 marketing message (what they promise) - Most common customer complaints (what they fail at) - Who they DON'T serve well Then synthesize: 1. THE GAP: What customer profile is underserved by ALL of them? 2. THE WEDGE: What's the one feature/promise that no one owns? 3. THE POSITION: Write a positioning statement for a new entrant that avoids all their weaknesses 4. BEACHHEAD CUSTOMER: Describe the ideal first 10 customers in 1 paragraph Format the competitive analysis as a table.

"We use Excel for this" tasks that happen at scale For each opportunity, estimate: - Number of businesses in Europe that would need this - Average revenue per customer - Realistic MRR ceiling for a niche tool - Barrier to entry (low/medium/high) and why End with: rank all opportunities by (Revenue potential × Low competition) score.

For each level, identify 3 SaaS ideas that capitalize on the trend being at [early/peak/mature] stage. Pricing Research Prompt
Открива правилния ценови модел и price point за дадена SaaS ниша.
Help me determine the right pricing strategy for a SaaS in the [niche] market. Research the following for this niche: 1. What do customers currently pay for adjacent tools? 2. What's the ROI they get (time saved, errors avoided, revenue gained)? 3. What pricing model do they prefer (flat/per-seat/usage)? 4. What's the psychological price ceiling (where they stop and ask their boss)? 5. What's the "no-brainer" price point (they sign up without needing approval)? Then recommend: - LAUNCH PRICE (low enough to get first 10 customers fast) - GROWTH PRICE (maximizes MRR per customer) - ENTERPRISE PRICE (for when they ask "do you have a bigger plan?") - Pricing page structure (how many tiers, what to include in each) - ONE pricing trick used by successful SaaS in adjacent niches Base your analysis on real pricing psychology, not guesses.

Full Go/No-Go Decision Framework
Цялостен framework за вземане на решение — строя ли или не?
You are a SaaS investor and operator who has built and exited 3 companies. Give me a structured GO / NO-GO decision on this idea. IDEA: [describe your idea] MY BACKGROUND: [your skills, available time, budget] TARGET MARKET: [geography, industry, company size] Evaluate across 7 dimensions (score 1-5 each): 1. HAIR ON FIRE: How urgently do customers need this? Evidence? 2. MARKET SIZE: How many paying customers exist in target geo? 3. WILLINGNESS TO PAY: What proof exists they pay for adjacent tools? 4. FOUNDER-MARKET FIT: Why are YOU the right person to build this? 5. BUILD FEASIBILITY: Can you ship v1 in 6 weeks, solo? 6. DISTRIBUTION: How do you reach the first 100 customers? 7. DEFENSIBILITY: In 2 years, why won't a big player kill you? SCORING: - 28-35: Build it immediately - 20-27: Build after validating #1 weakness - 12-19: Test the riskiest assumption first, don't build yet - Below 12: Kill it Give me total score + the one thing I must prove before writing a line of code.
Claude (best)
